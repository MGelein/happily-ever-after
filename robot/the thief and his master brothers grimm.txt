68 the thief and he Master BrothersS Grimm Hans wish to put he son to learn a trade , so he go into the church and pray to we Lord God to know which would be most advantageous for he . then the clerk get behind the altar , and say , `` Thieving , thieve . '' on this Hans go back to he son , and tell he he be to learn thieving , and that the Lord God have say so . so he go with he son to seek a man who be acquaint with thieve . they walk a long time and come into a great forest , where stand a little house with a old woman in it . Hans say , `` do you know of a man who be acquaint with thieve ? '' `` you can learn that here quite well , '' say the woman , `` my son be a master of it . '' so he speak with the son , and ask if he know thieve really well ? the master-thief say , `` I will teach he well . come back when a year be over , and then if you recognize you son , I will take no payment at all for teach he ; but if you do not know he , you must give I two hundred thaler . '' the father go home again , and the son learn witchcraft and thieving , thoroughly . when the year be out , the father be full of anxiety to know how he be to contrive to recognize he son . as he be thus go about in he trouble , he meet a little dwarf , who say , `` man , what ail you , that you be always in such trouble ? '' `` oh , '' say Hans , `` a year ago I place my son with a master-thief who tell I I be to come back when the year be out , and that if I then do not know my son when I see he , I be to pay two hundred thaler ; but if I do know he I be to pay nothing , and now I be afraid of not know he and can not tell where I be to get the money . '' then the dwarf tell he to take a small basket of bread with he , and to stand beneath the chimney . `` there on the cross-beam be a basket , out of which a little bird be peep , and that be you son . '' Hans go thither , and throw a little basket full of black bread in front of the basket with the bird in it , and the little bird come out , and look up . `` Hollo , my son , art thou here ? '' say the father , and the son be delighted to see he father , but the master-thief say , `` the devil must have prompt you , or how could you have know you son ? '' `` Father , let we go , '' say the youth . then the father and son set out homeward . on the way a carriage come drive by . Hereupon the son say to he father , `` I will change myself into a large greyhound , and then you can earn a great deal of money by I . '' then the gentleman call from the carriage , `` my man , will you sell you dog ? '' `` yes , '' say the father . `` how much do you want for it ? '' `` thirty thaler . '' `` Eh , man , that be a great deal , but as it be such a very fine dog I will have it . '' the gentleman take it into he carriage , but when they have drive a little farther the dog spring out of the carriage through the window , and go back to he father , and be no longer a greyhound . they go home together . next day there be a fair in the neighboring town , so the youth say to he father , `` I will now change myself into a beautiful horse , and you can sell I ; but when you have sell I , you must take off my bridle , or I can not become a man again . '' then the father go with the horse to the fair , and the master-thief come and buy the horse for a hundred thaler , but the father forget , and do not take off the bridle . so the man go home with the horse , and put it in the stable . when the maid cross the threshold , the horse say , `` take off my bridle , take off my bridle . '' then the maid stand still , and say , `` what , canst thou speak ? '' so she go and take the bridle off , and the horse become a sparrow , and fly out at the door , and the wizard become a sparrow also , and fly after he . then they come together and cast lot , but the master lose , and betake himself to the water and be a fish . then the youth also become a fish , and they cast lot again , and the master lose . so the master change himself into a cock , and the youth become a fox , and bite the master 's head off , and he die and have remain dead to this day .
