164 Lazy Harry BrothersS Grimm Harry be lazy , and although he have nothing else to do but drive he goat daily to pasture , he nevertheless groan when he go home after he day 's work be do . `` it be indeed a heavy burden , '' say he , `` and a wearisome employment to drive a goat into the field this way year after year , till late into the autumn ! if one could but lie down and sleep , but no , one must have one 's eye open lest it hurt the young tree , or squeeze itself through the hedge into a garden , or run away altogether . how can one have any rest , or peace of one 's life ? '' he seat himself , collect he thought , and consider how he could set he shoulder free from this burden . for a long time all thinking be to no purpose , but suddenly it be as if scale fall from he eye . `` I know what I will do , '' he cry , `` I will marry fat Trina who have also a goat , and can take mine out with hers , and then I shall have no more need to trouble myself . '' so Harry get up , set he weary leg in motion , and go right across the street , for it be no farther , to where the parent of fat Trina live , and ask for they industrious and virtuous daughter in marriage . the parent do not reflect long . `` bird of a feather , flock together , '' they think , and consent . so fat Trina become Harry 's wife , and lead out both the goat . Harry have a good time of it , and have no work that he require to rest from but he own idleness . he only go out with she now and then , and say , `` I merely do it that I may afterwards enjoy rest more , otherwise one lose all feeling for it . '' but fat Trina be no less idle . `` Dear Harry , '' say she one day , `` why should we make we life so toilsome when there be no need for it , and thus ruin the best day of we youth ? would it not be better for we to give the two goat which disturb we every morning in we sweetest sleep with they bleating , to we neighbor , and he will give we a beehive for they . we will put the beehive in a sunny place behind the house , and trouble ourselves no more about it . bee do not require to be take care of , or drive into the field ; they fly out and find the way home again for themselves , and collect honey without give the very least trouble . '' `` Thou hast speak like a sensible woman , '' reply Harry . `` we will carry out thy proposal without delay , and besides all that , honey taste better and nourish one better than goat 's milk , and it can be keep longer too . '' the neighbor willingly give a beehive for the two goat . the bee fly in and out from early morning till late evening without ever tire , and fill the hive with the most beautiful honey , so that in autumn Harry be able to take a whole pitcherful out of it . they place the jug on a board which be fix to the wall of they bed-room , and as they be afraid that it might be steal from they , or that the mouse might find it , Trina bring in a stout hazel-stick and put it beside she bed , so that without unnecessary get up she might reach it with she hand , and drive away the uninvited guest . Lazy Harry do not like to leave he bed before noon . `` he who rise early , '' say he , `` waste he substance . '' one morning when he be still lie amongst the feather in broad daylight , rest after he long sleep , he say to he wife , `` woman be fond of sweet thing , and thou art always tasting the honey in private ; it will be better for we to exchange it for a goose with a young gosling , before thou eatest up the whole of it . '' `` but , '' answer Trina , `` not before we have a child to take care of they ! Am I to worry myself with the little goose , and spend all my strength on they to no purpose . '' `` dost thou think , '' say Harry , `` that the youngster will look after goose ? now-a-days child no longer obey , they do accord to they own fancy , because they consider themselves cleverer than they parent , just like that lad who be send to seek the cow and chase three blackbird . '' `` oh , '' reply Trina , `` this one shall fare badly if he do not do what I say ! I will take a stick and belabor he skin for he with more blow than I can count . look , Harry , '' cry she in she zeal , and seize the stick which she have to drive the mouse away with , `` look , this be the way I will fall on he ! '' she reach she arm out to strike , but unhappily hit the honey-pitcher above the bed . the pitcher strike against the wall and fall down in fragment , and the fine honey stream down on the ground . `` there lie the goose and the young gosling , '' say Harry , `` and want no look after . but it be lucky that the pitcher do not fall on my head . we have all reason to be satisfy with we lot . '' and then as he see that there be still some honey in one of the fragment he stretch out he hand for it , and say quite gaily , `` the remain , my wife , we will still eat with a relish , and we will rest a little after the fright we have have . what matter if we do get up a little later the day be always long enough . '' `` yes , '' answer Trina , `` we shall always get to the end of it at the proper time . Dost thou know that the snail be once ask to a wedding and set out to go , but arrive at the christening . in front of the house it fall over the fence , and say , ` speed do no good . ' ''
