'' `` yes , '' say Hans , `` I will do all that . '' then the wooer put on a pair of old patched trousers , go to a rich peasant 's daughter in the next village , and say , `` Wo not you marry my nephew Hans -- you will get a honest and sensible man who will suit you ? '' the covetous father ask , `` how be it with regard to he means ? have he bread to break ? '' `` dear friend , '' reply the wooer , `` my young nephew have a snug berth , a nice bit of money in hand , and plenty of bread to break , besides he have quite as many patch as I have , '' -lrb-B- and as he speak , he slap the patch on he trousers , but in that district small piece of land be call patch also . -rrb-B- `` if you will give yourself the trouble to go home with I , you shall see at once that all be as I have say . '' then the miser do not want to lose this good opportunity , and say , `` if that be the case , I have nothing further to say against the marriage . '' so the wedding be celebrate on the appointed day , and when the young wife go out of door to see the bridegroom 's property , Hans take off he Sunday coat and put on he patched smock-frock and say , `` I might spoil my good coat . '' then together they go out and wherever a boundary line come in sight , or field and meadow be divide from each other , Hans point with he finger and then slap either a large or a small patch on he smock-frock , and say , `` that patch be mine , and that too , my dearest , just look at it , '' mean thereby that he wife should not stare at the broad land , but look at he garment , which be he own . `` be you indeed at the wedding ?
