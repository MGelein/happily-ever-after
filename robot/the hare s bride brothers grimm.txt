66 the Hare 's Bride BrothersS Grimm there be once a woman and she daughter who live in a pretty garden with cabbage ; and a little hare come into it , and during the winter time eat all the cabbage . then say the mother to the daughter , `` go into the garden , and chase the hare away . '' the girl say to the little hare , `` sh-sh , hare , you be still eat up all we cabbage . '' say the hare , `` come , maiden , and seat yourself on my little hare 's tail , and come with I into my little hare 's hut . '' the girl will not do it . next day the hare come again and eat the cabbage , then say the mother to the daughter , `` go into the garden , and drive the hare away . '' the girl say to the hare , `` sh-sh , little hare , you be still eat all the cabbage . '' the little hare say , `` Maiden , seat thyself on my little hare 's tail , and come with I into my little hare 's hut . '' the maiden refuse . the third day the hare come again , and eat the cabbage . on this the mother say to the daughter , `` go into the garden , and hunt the hare away . '' say the maiden , `` sh-sh , little hare , you be still eat all we cabbage . '' say the little hare , `` come , maiden , seat thyself on my little hare 's tail , and come with I into my little hare 's hut . '' the girl seat herself on the little hare 's tail , and then the hare take she far away to he little hut , and say , `` now cook green cabbage and millet-seed , and I will invite the wedding-guests . '' then all the wedding-guests assemble . -lrb-B- who be the wedding-guests ? -rrb-B- that I can tell you as another tell it to I . they be all hare , and the crow be there as parson to marry the bride and bridegroom , and the fox as clerk , and the altar be under the rainbow . the girl , however , be sad , for she be all alone . the little hare come and say , `` open the door , open the door , the wedding-guests be merry . '' the bride say nothing , but weep . the little hare go away . the little hare come back and say , `` take off the lid , take off the lid , the wedding-guests be hungry . '' the bride again say nothing , and weep . the little hare go away . the little hare come back and say , `` take off the lid , take off the lid , the wedding-guests be wait . '' then the bride say nothing , and the hare go away , but she dress a straw-doll in she clothes , and give she a spoon to stir with , and set she by the pan with the millet-seed , and go back to she mother . the little hare come once more and say , `` take off the lid , take off the lid , '' and get up , and strike the doll on the head so that she cap fall off . then the little hare see that it be not he bride , and go away and be sorrowful .
