116 the Blue Light BrothersS Grimm there be once on a time a soldier who for many year have serve the King faithfully , but when the war come to a end could serve no longer because of the many wound which he have receive . the King say to he , `` Thou mayst return to thy home , I need thee no longer , and thou wilt not receive any more money , for he only receive wages who render I service for they . '' then the soldier do not know how to earn a living , go away greatly trouble , and walk the whole day , until in the evening he enter a forest . when darkness come on , he see a light , which he go up to , and come to a house wherein live a witch . `` do give I one night 's lodging , and a little to eat and drink , '' say he to she , `` or I shall starve . '' `` Oho ! '' she answer , `` who give anything to a run-away soldier ? yet will I be compassionate , and take you in , if you will do what I wish . '' `` what do you wish ? '' say the soldier . `` that you should dig all round my garden for I , tomorrow . '' the soldier consent , and next day labor with all he strength , but could not finish it by the evening . `` I see well enough , '' say the witch , `` that you can do no more to-day , but I will keep you yet another night , in payment for which you must to-morrow chop I a load of wood , and make it small . '' the soldier spend the whole day in do it , and in the evening the witch propose that he should stay one night more . `` to-morrow , you shall only do I a very trifle piece of work . behind my house , there be a old dry well , into which my light have fall , it burn blue , and never go out , and you shall bring it up again for I . '' next day the old woman take he to the well , and let he down in a basket . he find the blue light , and make she a signal to draw he up again . she do draw he up , but when he come near the edge , she stretch down she hand and want to take the blue light away from he . `` no , '' say he , perceive she evil intention , `` I will not give thee the light until I be stand with both foot upon the ground . '' the witch fall into a passion , let he down again into the well , and go away . the poor soldier fall without injury on the moist ground , and the blue light go on burning , but of what use be that to he ? he see very well that he could not escape death . he sit for a while very sorrowfully , then suddenly he feel in he pocket and find he tobacco pipe , which be still half full . `` this shall be my last pleasure , '' think he , pull it out , light it at the blue light and begin to smoke . when the smoke have circle about the cavern , suddenly a little black dwarf stand before he , and say , `` Lord , what be thy command ? '' `` what command have I to give thee ? '' reply the soldier , quite astonished . `` I must do everything thou biddest I , '' say the little man . `` good , '' say the soldier ; `` then in the first place help I out of this well . '' the little man take he by the hand , and lead he through a underground passage , but he do not forget to take the blue light with he . on the way the dwarf show he the treasure which the witch have collect and hide there , and the soldier take as much gold as he could carry . when he be above , he say to the little man , `` now go and bind the old witch , and carry she before the judge . '' in a short time she , with frightful cry , come ride by , as swift as the wind on a wild tom-cat , nor be it long after that before the little man re-appeared . `` it be all do , '' say he , `` and the witch be already hang on the gallows . what further command have my lord ? '' inquire the dwarf . `` at this moment , none , '' answer the soldier ; `` Thou canst return home , only be at hand immediately , if I summon thee . '' `` nothing more be need than that thou shouldst light thy pipe at the blue light , and I will appear before thee at once . '' Thereupon he vanish from he sight . the soldier return to the town from which he have come . he go to the best inn , order himself handsome clothes , and then bid the landlord furnish he a room as handsomely as possible . when it be ready and the soldier have take possession of it , he summon the little black mannikin and say , `` I have serve the King faithfully , but he have dismiss I , and leave I to hunger , and now I want to take my revenge . '' `` what be I to do ? '' ask the little man . `` late at night , when the King 's daughter be in bed , bring she here in she sleep , she shall do servant 's work for I . '' the mannikin say , `` that be a easy thing for I to do , but a very dangerous thing for you , for if it be discover , you will fare ill. '' when twelve o'clock have strike , the door spring open , and the mannikin carry in the princess . `` aha ! art thou there ? '' cry the soldier , `` get to thy work at once ! fetch the broom and sweep the chamber . '' when she have do this , he order she to come to he chair , and then he stretch out he foot and say , `` pull off my boot for I , '' and then he throw they in she face , and make she pick they up again , and clean and brighten they . she , however , do everything he bid she , without opposition , silently and with half-shut eye . when the first cock crow , the mannikin carry she back to the royal palace , and lay she in she bed . next morning when the princess arise , she go to she father , and tell he that she have have a very strange dream . `` I be carry through the street with the rapidity of lightning , '' say she , `` and take into a soldier 's room , and I have to wait upon he like a servant , sweep he room , clean he boot , and do all kind of menial work . it be only a dream , and yet I be just as tire as if I really have do everything . '' `` the dream may have be true , '' say the King , `` I will give thee a piece of advice . fill thy pocket full of pea , and make a small hole in it , and then if thou art carry away again , they will fall out and leave a track in the street . '' but unseen by the King , the mannikin be stand beside he when he say that , and hear all . at night when the sleep princess be again carry through the street , some pea certainly do fall out of she pocket , but they make no track , for the crafty mannikin have just before scattered pea in every street there be . and again the princess be compel to do servant 's work until cock-crow . next morning the King send he people out to seek the track , but it be all in vain , for in every street poor child be sit , pick up pea , and say , `` it must have rain pea , last night . '' `` we must think of something else , '' say the King ; `` keep thy shoe on when thou goest to bed , and before thou comest back from the place where thou art take , hide one of they there , I will soon contrive to find it . '' the black mannikin hear this plot , and at night when the soldier again order he to bring the princess , reveal it to he , and tell he that he know of no expedient to counteract this stratagem , and that if the shoe be find in the soldier 's house it would go badly with he . `` do what I bid thee , '' reply the soldier , and again this third night the princess be oblige to work like a servant , but before she go away , she hide she shoe under the bed . next morning the King have the entire town search for he daughter 's shoe . it be find at the soldier 's , and the soldier himself , who at the entreaty of the dwarf have go outside the gate , be soon bring back , and throw into prison . in he flight he have forget the most valuable thing he have , the blue light and the gold , and have only one ducat in he pocket . and now load with chain , he be stand at the window of he dungeon , when he chance to see one of he comrade pass by . the soldier tap at the pane of glass , and when this man come up , say to he , `` be so kind as to fetch I the small bundle I have leave lie in the inn , and I will give you a ducat for do it . '' he comrade run thither and bring he what he want . as soon as the soldier be alone again , he light he pipe and summon the black mannikin . `` have no fear , '' say the latter to he master . `` go wheresoever they take you , and let they do what they will , only take the blue light with you . '' next day the soldier be try , and though he have do nothing wicked , the judge condemn he to death . when he be lead forth to die , he beg a last favor of the King . `` what be it ? '' ask the King . `` that I may smoke one more pipe on my way . '' `` Thou mayst smoke three , '' answer the King , `` but do not imagine that I will spare thy life . '' then the soldier pull out he pipe and light it at the blue light , and as soon as a few wreath of smoke have ascend , the mannikin be there with a small cudgel in he hand , and say , `` what do my lord command ? '' `` strike down to earth that false judge there , and he constable , and spare not the king who have treat I so ill. '' then the mannikin fall on they like lightning , dart this way and that way , and whosoever be so much as touch by he cudgel fall to earth , and do not venture to stir again . the King be terrify ; he throw himself on the soldier 's mercy , and merely to be allow to live at all , give he he kingdom for he own , and the princess to wife .
